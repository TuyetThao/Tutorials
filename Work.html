IT WORK


Game Programmer


Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e_TxH59MclA&ab_channel=PaolofromTOKYO


Masa (23 years old) is a Game Programmer in Tokyo - Japan


He is working at Bandai Namco Inc. which is one of the biggest game company in Japan


At 10 a.m, when coming to his desk at the company, the first thing he does is checking email and internal company social network for updating today’s tasks. He always has a fast breakfast right on his desk because he does not want to waste his time. After finishing his breakfast, he spends all the morning coding his task. At 12 p.m, he often hangs out with his co-workers to have lunch in nearby restaurants or convenience stores and plays some games for entertainment. At 1 p.m, he keeps up with his code, he also has some meeting with his team if necessary. In the meeting, he will give some suggestions for the game operation, future plan as well as persuade all team members to accept his idea. After that, he will present this plan to game directors in another meeting. If his plan is confirmed, he will start to do it as the new tasks with his team. He often ends his work around 7 p.m to 8 p.m depend on his recent situation. With Masa, I think his most challenging work comes from coding, we can see that he needs too much concentration for this work and he must find something to relax after working with it. Coding is also his capture of work that shows the essence of the IT industry.


Frontend Engineer


Source: https://engineering.zalando.com/posts/2019/01/frontend-engineer-zalando.html


Cristiano Correia is a Frontend Engineer in Berlin – Germany


He is working at Zalando SE which is a European e-commerce company.


As a front-end developer, he requires always to put the user first, he also needs to have a lot of experience as a user to draw the experience and bring the best user experience. Every day, he is a problem solver, looking for problems to improve them with the back-end developer. It is important to work with Javascript all day long while constantly updating and learning their new improvements, learning the languages, core functions of each different programming language, understanding the nature (asynchronous/synchronous) and its browser APIs. Recheck all the codes that he had done, he always makes questions for himself like “do this code performant?” if not he will make it change. Although he uses Javascript to be able to design faster, he doesn't because he wants to create a website that he designs and fits in with what the UI / UX designer has to offer. His mission is to create a website that is meaningful and content well that ensures our target customer is understandable and that any co-workers look at it and know what they should do next. To him, CSS is very important because it helps to keep the website clean and efficient.


Continuous learning is something that he and his colleagues always have to do, constantly spend time learning and planning future projects. Attention to the quality and performance of the webpage is judged to be his success. Put the customer's interests first and prevent something from going wrong. Don't be afraid to experiment and run operational, project settings from the simplest to the most complete. Know how to use multiple Agile methods, share knowledge with colleagues, grow together for the mutual benefit of the team. He is encouraged by the company to share knowledge in the community, being international to integrate into different cultures so that the website development must be suitable for all users with many languages in the world. His challenge is to always have a new idea, catch up with the trend and create a webpage suitable for today's consumers. Besides, he always has to find the most effective ways to improve the quality and performance of the website.


UX Designer


Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9DOOeX8sVVc&ab_channel=Hello%2CI%27mAlexa


Alexa is a UX Designer in San Francisco – USA


She is working at Zendesk Inc. which is an American customer service software company.


She starts a day at 6 am, doing her morning routine and at 8:30 am she has a meeting with her friends, her family all over the world. At 9:30 she gets the bus to go to the office, and she comes to the office at 10 am. At 10:15 am, she starts seeing her schedule of her day and starts checking her emails. At 11:30 am she has a team stand up to talk about what her team did last week, what her team will do this week, talk about things that clocking her team, figure out what to do to keep moving forward. Then, she read through the project documentation or read relevant articles that her teammate sent about design. She starts to create her own project documentation which coming from different types of design files, digital pages like in confluence or slide decks, … Then, she has her lunch at 12:00 pm. Back to work and she has a lot of meeting throughout the day because their a lot of work that needs to be communicated with teammates. She listens to all people who have different backgrounds, have different perspectives to make a decision for the project. Continuing with other meetings for weekly critiques as well as her core team which pans across all over the world. She also holds critique sessions with her engineering and product manager partners and then she goes to the mini feedback session. There is a lot of meeting, the presentation that she needs to be done, sometimes she does the X research with customers or international stakeholders. Sometimes, she will have a UX workshop to gain her knowledge. She often ends her day at 8 pm and she goes to play basketball. She found challenging to do headstand design work but she knows these meetings are super important and necessary for different people on the team to talk about the product.


Backend Foundation Engineer


Source: https://slack.engineering/a-day-in-the-life-of-a-backend-foundation-engineer-at-slack/


Maude Lemaire is a Backend Foundation Engineer in San Francisco – USA


She is working at Slack Technologies which is an American software company.


Waking up at 6 am, starting her morning routine, and she comes to her office at 7:15 am. She starts her work by opening her GitHub Enterprise and dive into it. Her company is second only to Facebook as the biggest Hacklacng and HHVM users, so she and her team have the task of upgrading their language stack. This is a rapidly evolving language and it gets small version updates almost every week. Her team must ensure that they are always up to date with the latest versions when applying the latest language features. Most of the time, her team can automate those changes, but with more complex, more skillful changes they are forced to handle them manually. 8 am is her time to enjoy a cup of coffee, and chat with people who drink coffee early. Then she goes back to work, arranges the hardest parts of the day to take care of first, records the hard-to-handle errors, and starts fixing them. At 10 a.m, she started teaching Slack beginners the basics of Slack, their entire system, knowledge of HTTPS and their web portal. At 1 pm, she has a meeting to discuss the past week's progress and plans for the next week. At 2 pm, she will have an afternoon coffee with other engineers to build strong relationships with everyone in the company, raise everyone's morale to get the job done efficiently. 3 pm is the time for her to reply to the email to answer questions and deal with code or conflicts that staff cannot handle. She starts taking notes on unfinished work, noting ideas to solve problems so that everything is prepared the next morning. She ends her day at 5 pm and goes home with her family.

Kaya is a Data Scientist at a start-up company in South Korea


Her work is analyzing data and reporting them, for example, when another project team from the company released a new engine or product, she will check and follow the data of these products during the week to make sure nothing unreasonable data appears. If she finds some issues with the data, she can also revise the matching algorithm. Until finding all the issues, and revising them, she needs to have a data report meeting with that project team. In the meeting, she needs to show her logical thinking, what she changed in the algorithm and why she changed it. Since the whole group agreed, they focus on the schedule to release the product. These works cycle just keep it like that every week. She continues with a new project after having a meeting with the CEO, she will receive the project progress as well as the action plans. Then she and her data team will analyze this project and separate it into small tasks. Finally, before going home, she checks messages of all teams, feedback from customers, and lists tomorrow’s tasks. Her job revolves around statistics, algorithm design, data problem solving, reporting results, and ensuring the product's data circuit works stably. With Kaya, a designing algorithm for the project is the most challenged work because she needs to imagine all the workflow of this project and go deep inside each feature. Working with data is her capture of work that shows the essence of the IT industry.
